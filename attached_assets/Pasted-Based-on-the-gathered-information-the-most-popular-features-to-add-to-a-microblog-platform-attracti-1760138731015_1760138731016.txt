Based on the gathered information, the most popular features to add to a microblog platform attracting users of X (formerly Twitter), Blogger, and Substack include:

### Popular Microblogging Features
- **Short-form content**: Users prefer bite-sized posts, short sentences, images, or video clips for quick consumption and real-time updates.[1][3][5]
- **Real-time interaction**: Features that enable live updates, quick reactions, and instantaneous sharing are crucial.[3][1]
- **Multimedia support**: Integration of images, videos, audio (like podcasting/audio narration), and GIFs.[4][3]
- **Hashtags and trending topics**: To categorize content and increase discoverability.[3]
- **Audience engagement tools**: Polls, comments, questions, and prompts to foster interaction.[2][4]
- **Private community/Subscriber chat**: Private group discussions or AMAs with subscribers to build loyalty.[2]
- **Subscription features**: Options for paid content, pledges, reader-only posts, and series are popular on platforms like Substack.[2]
- **Custom branding**: Custom domains and personalized welcome emails that make the platform feel like a personal brand extension.[2]
- **Content organization**: Good navigation with categories, tags, and layouts that make it feel like a blog.[4]
- **Advanced analytics**: Detailed analytics dashboards to track engagement, subscription trends, clicks, and open rates.[2]

These features reflect what users from these platforms value, combining fast real-time microblogging with community-building and subscription monetization seen in Substack and traditional blogging features on Blogger.
